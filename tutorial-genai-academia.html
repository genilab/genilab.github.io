Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences at FAU images/icons/genilab-knowledge.png TALK - Generative Intelligence in Academic Reseaerch This talk (~ 1-hour) introduces the core concepts, capabilities, and practical applications of Generative Intelligence in scientific research. It is designed for a non-technical audience and aims to promote an understanding of Generative AI as an enabler for new forms of **interaction, collaboration, and creativity** in research. * What is Generative Intelligence, and how does it differ from traditional AI approaches? * How can GenAI support scientific research for non-technical users? * How can Generative AI assist in analyzing and interpreting results? * How can scientists begin exploring GenAI tools (beyond chatbots)?

Target Audience

This talk is intended for faculty, researchers, and students from non-technical backgrounds who are interested in exploring the role of Generative Intelligence in scientific research.
